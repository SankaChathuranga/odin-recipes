Lets make Biriyani

Kottu, a street-food favorite in Sri Lanka, is made by stir-frying shredded flatbread with vegetables, meat, eggs, and a mix of spices.
The rhythmic chopping of the ingredients on a hot griddle gives kottu its signature texture and flavor, making it a hearty and satisfying dish.

Kottu Recipe

  • Flatbread: 4 pieces (roti or paratha, shredded)
  • Meat or Vegetables: 250g (chicken, beef, or tofu, cooked and shredded)
  • Eggs: 2 (optional)
  • Vegetables: Carrots, cabbage, and leeks (shredded)
  • Onion: 1 medium (sliced)
  • Garlic: 2 cloves (minced)
  • Curry Powder: 1 tablespoon
  • Soy Sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Chili Paste: 1 teaspoon (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and Pepper: To taste
  • Oil: For cooking

Recipe Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large pan or griddle.
  2. Add sliced onions, minced garlic, and shredded vegetables. Stir-fry until softened.
  3. Add the cooked and shredded meat or tofu. Mix well.
  4. Season with curry powder, soy sauce, chili paste, salt, and pepper. Cook for 2 minutes.
  5. Push the mixture to one side of the pan and scramble the eggs (if using) on the other side.
  6. Mix the eggs with the rest of the ingredients.
  7. Add the shredded flatbread and toss everything together until evenly coated with spices.
  8. Serve hot with curry or sauce on the side.
